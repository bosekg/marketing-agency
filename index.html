08

Bottlenecks to design around

RISK 01Proof concentration
Three engines means you need credible proof in three categories before any one looks legit. Spread the first clients too thin and you're "the agency for X" in none.
Move: track case studies per engine from day one and double down where traction shows first — let proof decide, not a pre-picked favorite.
RISK 02Context-switching tax
Each engine is a different stack and metric — Commerce is campaign-burst, Concierge is ongoing ops, product design is deep founder-brain work. Flipping daily erodes quality.
Move: batch by engine in blocks; write the SOP for an engine before selling the second client in it.
RISK 03Concierge unit economics
Voice-AI lead capture is usually priced per-lead / per-minute, so cost-to-serve scales with the client's volume. A high-volume client can become the least profitable one.
Move: make the voice cost a pass-through or volume-tier — don't absorb it into a flat retainer.
RISK 04Product design throughput
It's the least systematizable thing on offer and it lives in your heads. One demanding coach can stall the whole shop.
Move: cap concurrent builds; scope product design as a fixed high-ticket sprint with a hard deliverable boundary.
RISK 05Dashboard build cost & GEO timeline
The dashboard has to show three metric sets (sales/ROAS, applications, booked appointments). And GEO results build over months, not days.
Move: build the dashboard modular (one shell, three metric templates). Position GEO as a long-game layer — and run it on Diamant itself first.
