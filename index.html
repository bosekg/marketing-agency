Positioning — what we are

One-liner
We build the full acquisition machine for high-ticket brands — from the first ad impression to the booked call or the sale.

Not a video shop. Not a "we run ads" agency. We own both ends of the funnel — the creative that gets attention and the system that converts it — and we use AI to do it faster and cheaper than a traditional agency can.

We are
  • A productized AI/marketing agency: two clear "engines" + an automation upsell
  • Outcome sellers — sales and booked appointments, not "deliverables"
  • Owners of the whole funnel: creative → page/lead-catcher → backend
  • Fast: AI collapses creative cost and follow-up speed
We are not
  • A one-off "make me a video" service
  • A generic "AI will transform your business" pitch
  • A retainer-for-vibes agency with no defined outcome
  • Trying to serve everyone — high-ticket only, two ICPs only
02

The market insight — two leaks

It's almost never a traffic problem. High-ticket brands lose money in two predictable places. Our whole business is built on closing both — which is also why our two offers feel like one product line, not two random services.

Leak 01 — before the click
The creative bottleneck
Paid traffic eats creative. To keep CPAs down you need fresh, scroll-stopping video constantly. A traditional agency charges thousands per ad and takes weeks, so clients run the same 3 tired ads until they fatigue.
Leak 02 — after the click
The lead leak
The lead comes in and then goes cold. Average follow-up across industries is 40+ hours, and roughly a third of leads are never contacted at all. A faster competitor books the deal first.
Our wedge: AI is the first thing that fixes both leaks at once — it collapses the cost of producing creative AND the cost of instant follow-up. We sit on both ends. That's the moat and the message.
03

Offers

Two engines run on the same AI creative. The difference is what happens after the click — selling a product or booking a call. Then one upsell once they're in.

Engine 01
Primary · validated direction
The Storefront Engine
For ecom stores & coaches who sell direct (no call needed)
What's included
  • → AI video ads at volume (UGC / product / hook-driven)
  • → Media buying & account management
  • → Conversion-built webshop / landing page
  • → Continuous creative testing loop
The promise
Click → purchase, with no human in the loop. Lower CPA through constant fresh creative the client could never produce at this speed alone.
Deliverable
A live ad account + a page built to convert, refreshed on a cadence.
Engine 02
In validation
The Booking Engine
For high-ticket service providers who close on appointments
What's included
  • → AI video ads at volume (tuned for lead-gen)
  • → AI lead-catcher: instant response to every form fill
  • → Qualification + routing to calendar
  • → Booked appointments handed to the client
The promise
Booked, qualified appointments — not raw leads. Speed-to-lead stops being the bottleneck.
Status note
Lead-catcher mechanics not concluded. See Open decisions (07) before selling this hard.
Upsell
Existing clients only
Backend automations
Sold once the front-end is working and trust is built
What's included
  • → Follow-up & nurture sequences
  • → CRM hygiene & pipeline automation
  • → Client onboarding flows
  • → Reporting & internal ops automation
Why it's an upsell, not a lead offer
Hard to sell cold — needs trust and access to their systems. Easy to sell once we've already made them money. Higher margin, stickier retention.

ICP profiles

So we both pitch the same person the same way. The common thread: high margin per sale — which is what makes a leaked impression or a cold lead actually expensive.

"My ads are stale, CPMs keep creeping up, and I can't shoot new creative fast enough to keep the account fresh. I'm scaling spend on tired ads."

Rising CPA · creative fatigue · launching a new product/offer · just got burned by a slow/expensive agency.

Open decisions — settle these

The things we haven't concluded. Don't sell around these until we've decided.

▸ Does the AI lead-catcher stay in the offer — and in what form?
Channel: SMS vs. voice AI vs. web chat? How deep does qualification go before a human takes over? Where's the handoff? A robotic or mistimed response can burn a high-ticket lead worse than a slow human — design the failure modes first.
▸ Pricing & packaging model
Setup fee + monthly retainer? Performance / per-booked-appointment? Per-engine pricing? Need a number we can both quote with confidence. [ fill ]
▸ Fulfillment capacity
How many clients can we actually deliver for at once before quality drops? What's the cap before we hire or systematize harder? [ fill ]
▸ Lead the funnel with one engine or both?
Engine 01 is more validated. Do we focus all outbound on it first and treat Engine 02 as a referral/expansion play, or run both from day one?
